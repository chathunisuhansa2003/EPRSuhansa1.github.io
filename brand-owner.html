You are a Brand Owner

If you own a brand and place products bearing your trademark on the Sri Lankan market — even if you do not manufacture them yourself — you are classified as a Brand Owner under EPR rules.

Who is a Brand Owner?

A Brand Owner is any entity that:

  • Sells products under its own brand name or trademark
  • Commissions or licenses the production of goods marketed under its brand
  • Is the first entity to introduce branded products to the Sri Lankan market
  • Uses its logo/name on packaging or the product itself (even if manufactured by a third party)

Even if you do not physically produce the items, as the brand owner you are considered the **producer** for EPR purposes and carry the legal responsibility for the post-consumer waste generated by your branded products.

Your EPR Responsibilities

Under Sri Lanka’s Extended Producer Responsibility framework, Brand Owners must:

  • Register with the designated Producer Responsibility Organization (PRO)
  • Organize or finance collection, take-back, and recycling systems for end-of-life products
  • Meet national or category-specific collection and recovery targets
  • Submit annual reports on products placed on the market and waste managed
  • Ensure proper labeling and provide information to consumers about return/recycling options
